Rassenkunde

Der Begriff „Rasse“

Die Natur bietet scheinbar das Bild eines unübersehbaren Durcheinanders. Ein kleines Stück Land ernährt eine Unzahl von Pflanzen und Tieren, ein kleiner See birgt unendlich viele Lebewesen. Hinter der Vielfalt der Formen steht jedoch eine Ordnung, die nach ganz bestimmten Gesetzen die einzelnen Arten in der Tier- und Pflanzenwelt voneinander trennt. Nie würden sich Hund und Affe ober Eiche und Linde untereinander vermischen. Jede Art ist innerlich abgeschlossen von der anderen. Ihre Eigenart vererbt sie an ihre Nachkommen weiter. Innerhalb dieser Arten gibt es wiederum Gruppen, die Rassen. Auch sie folgen denselben Gesetzen wie jene. Nur reinrassige Lebewesen sind schön und harmonisch gebaut und sin auch wirklich wertvoll. Eine Rasse ist also in sich erbgleiche Menschengruppe. Innerhalb einer Rasse müssen die Menschen in allen Anlagen übereinstimmen, und diese Eigenschaften vererben sie weiter. Die Gesamtheit der Erbanlagen nennen wir „Erbmasse“.

Racial Science

The Term "Race"

Nature seems to offer the picture of an unmissable mess. A small piece of land feeds a myriad of plants and animals, a small lake harbors an infinite number of creatures. Behind the variety of forms, however, there is an order that separates the individual species in the animal and plant world from one another according to very specific laws. A dog and a monkey would never mix with each other above oak and linden tree. Each species is internally isolated from the other. She passes her personality on to her offspring. Within these species there are in turn groups, the races. They too follow the same laws as they do. Only purebred creatures are beautifully and harmoniously built and are really valuable. The half-breed, the bastard, is in any case inferior. Nature soon eliminates him, either because he succumbs to strong, healthy creatures or because he is unable to reproduce.

The human species is also divided into different races. According to Günther, we understand the human race to be a group of people "which differs from every other group of people (combined in such a way) through their own combination of physical characteristics and mental characteristics and only produces their own kind over and over again." A race is a group of people with the same hereditary status. Within a race, people must agree in all traits, and these traits are passed on. We call the entirety of the hereditary assets “genetic assets.”

There are no longer any races that correspond to the pure scientific concept, but only mixtures of races, the peoples. In Europe, the individual peoples that we call "whites" only differ from each other in the mix of races. Their peculiarity is based on the predominance of a certain breed. All the main European races are also represented in Germany: the Nordic, Eastern, Dinaric, Eastern Baltic and Western; in addition there are touches of the Falic and southern races as well as some foreign races.
